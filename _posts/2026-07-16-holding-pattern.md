### This week has been quite a lot

**I'm feeling all mush brained** after taking on a new role at work and the ensuing whirlwind week of training, meeting new people, and taking on a completely new work flow. So here's a song I've been listening to a lot lately.

<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/upJ3OgMRiUA?si=xcw7JbDWIMATa7J8" title="YouTube video player" frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture; web-share" referrerpolicy="strict-origin-when-cross-origin" allowfullscreen></iframe>

Something about Dinah's smokey tremolo, the cool trailing snare, ponderous bass, with the light guitar, and airy strings and vocals...

The song is a straightforward new romance story, but I think the message fits another context. In general, life is always changing. We can be happy or sad, or experiencing any of a myriad of different things, and given a few moments or 24 hours, well, oh what a difference.

I've been thinking a lot about the title of my earlier post, "What does it mean to be from somewhere?" There is a story in that phrase, and it started burbling to the surface last night. So I'm going to go tinker with that. But first, I need to read some Dutch. 

Enjoy the song.
